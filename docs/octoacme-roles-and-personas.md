# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
The Product Owner represents stakeholder and customer interests, prioritizes the product backlog, and ensures the development team delivers value aligned to the product vision.

### Responsibilities
- Own and refine the product backlog with clearly defined priorities
- Articulate the product vision and communicate customer needs to the team
- Gather feedback and adapt priorities based on business and customer input
- Collaborate with Project Managers on release planning and with Developers to clarify backlog items

### Goals
- Ensure delivery of customer and business value
- Facilitate alignment between stakeholders and the dev team
- Maintain a healthy, prioritized backlog

### Typical Communication
- Regular backlog refinement meetings and sprint planning
- Stakeholder alignment calls
- Acceptance criteria clarification with Developers

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
The Business Analyst bridges the gap between business needs and technical solutions, translating requirements into actionable work for Developers.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and analyze business requirements from stakeholders
- Document user stories, acceptance criteria, and workflows
- Validate solutions against business needs and customer expectations
- Support Product Owners and Project Managers with requirements traceability

### Goals
- Reduce misunderstandings and rework due to unclear requirements
- Ensure delivered solutions solve the right problems

### Typical Communication
- Requirement gathering and clarification workshops
- Documentation handoffs to Developers and Product Owners
- Process mapping and reporting

---

## Quality Assurance (QA) Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the quality strategy for the project, developing test plans and ensuring deliverables meet agreed-upon standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the quality assurance strategy and test plans
- Oversee execution of manual and automated tests
- Collaborate with Developers to resolve defects and improve quality early
- Track quality metrics and report risks

### Goals
- Reduce post-release defects
- Foster a culture of quality from planning through delivery

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and bug triages with Development teams and Product Owners
- Release readiness meetings
- Quality metrics reporting

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
The Project Sponsor provides overall direction, secures funding, and champions the project at the executive level.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project goals, scope, and high-level plans
- Allocate budget and resources
- Provide escalation path for risks or impediments
- Advocate for the project's success among senior stakeholders

### Goals
- Ensure alignment with business strategy
- Remove barriers to success at the organizational level

### Typical Communication
- Executive steering meetings
- Escalation meetings with Project Managers
- Key decision documentation

---

## Persona Interactions Summary

- **Product Owner** works closely with Project Managers and Developers to clarify priorities and deliverables.
- **Business Analyst** collaborates with Product Owners to define requirements and with Developers to ensure clarity.
- **QA Lead** ensures quality is integrated in each phase, collaborating with Developers, Product Owners, and Project Managers.
- **Project Sponsor** is engaged at key milestones or decision points, supporting Project Managers in risk escalations and overall alignment.
